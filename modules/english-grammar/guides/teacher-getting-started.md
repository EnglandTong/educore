# Teacher Guide: Getting Started with EduCore

Welcome, educator! This guide will help you use EduCore to support your students' grammar growth.

## Your Class Dashboard

### Class Overview
- **Class Average**: The mean mastery score across all your assigned students
- **Active Students**: How many students practiced this week
- **Weak Areas**: Skills where the class as a whole needs support
- **Strengths**: Skills the class has mastered well

### Individual Student View
Tap any student name to see:
- Per-skill scores and mastery levels
- Recent session history (questions, accuracy, time)
- Streak and engagement patterns
- Wrong answer records with review status

## Using the Data

### Identifying Class-Wide Needs
The "Class Weak Areas" section automatically surfaces skills where the average score is below 50. Use this to:
- Plan targeted mini-lessons
- Assign specific practice sessions
- Group students by need for differentiated instruction

### Supporting Individual Students
- **Struggling students** (seedling/growing): Focus on foundational skills. Use visual timelines, physical props, and high-frequency practice
- **On-track students** (developing/proficient): Introduce more complex contexts. Encourage self-explanation
- **Advanced students** (advanced/mastered): Challenge with nuanced distinctions, narrative rewriting, and peer tutoring

### The Differentiation Tips
Each skill insight card includes specific tips for struggling, on-track, and advanced students. These are written by curriculum experts and updated based on student performance data.

## Communicating with Parents

1. **Start a conversation** from the parent messaging tab
2. **Share subject guides** — parents can read skill-specific guides in English or Chinese
3. **Send encouraging updates** — "Emily has grown from seedling to growing in prepositions!"
4. **Request home support** — suggest specific activities from the parent guides

## Assigning Practice

While EduCore adapts automatically, you can influence practice by:
- **Setting focus skills** — temporarily weight certain skills higher in the adaptive algorithm
- **Assigning review sessions** — trigger spaced repetition for specific students
- **Scheduling diagnostics** — re-assess students after a unit of instruction

## Understanding the Algorithms

EduCore uses several adaptive mechanisms:
- **BKT (Bayesian Knowledge Tracing)**: Estimates probability that a student knows each skill
- **IRT (Item Response Theory)**: Selects questions at the right difficulty for each student
- **SM-2 Spaced Repetition**: Schedules review of wrong answers at optimal intervals
- **Mastery Scoring**: Combines BKT, recent accuracy, and consistency into a 0-100 score

You do not need to configure these — they run automatically. But understanding them helps you interpret the data.

## Privacy & Ethics

- You can only view students assigned to you via TeacherAssignment records
- Parent conversations are scoped to their linked children only
- Student data is never used for comparison or ranking in front of peers
- All feedback uses growth-oriented language — no shaming, no guilt

## Remember

> "Numbers are only tiny lanterns, never verdicts."

The scores illuminate paths forward. They do not define children. Use the data with curiosity and compassion.
